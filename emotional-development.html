Building a business with your partner is one of the most powerful things you can do — and one of the most complex. Keeping your CEO/co-founder dynamic separate from your personal relationship requires deliberate structure. Not a one-time conversation — an ongoing practice.

Assertive communication

As CEO you'll need to deliver hard messages — to clients who aren't renewing, to employees who aren't performing, to investors who are pushing back. Assertive (not aggressive, not passive) communication is a learnable skill. FP Advance and the Intelliflo conference are live practice grounds.

Early-stage startup life is almost entirely ambiguous — no clear path, no guaranteed outcomes, constant pivoting. The emotional ability to act decisively without certainty is what separates founders who survive year two from those who don't.

High standards and self-compassion aren't opposites — the best performers have both. Staying at Lucent when it's emotionally draining requires forgiving yourself on the hard days without using it as a reason to lower the bar.
