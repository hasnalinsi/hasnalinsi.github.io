Personal brand

The story of who you are needs to be consistent across every touchpoint. LinkedIn, conference introductions, how you write emails, how you show up in a room. You are already building this. The FP Advance speech and the Robin Powell interview are the foundation.

LinkedIn profile positioned as FCA compliance expert and future founder, not just employee
Post once a week on FCA compliance, Consumer Duty, or IFA industry topics, build authority before launch
Prepare a 2-minute founder story, why Amaea, why you, why now. Practised until it is effortless
Speaking reputation established, FP Advance, CISI talk already done
◆

Appearance & presence

How you look and carry yourself signals how you see yourself before you have said a word. A CEO's appearance is a deliberate choice, not vanity. Authoritative, polished, and entirely yours.

Define a signature style, a consistent aesthetic that reads as confident and professional in any room
Conference-ready wardrobe, know exactly what you wear to FP Advance, Intelliflo, CISI events
Physical presence, posture, eye contact, pacing. The gym is part of this. How you hold yourself changes how a room receives you
Headshots updated, professional photos that match the CEO you are becoming, not the employee you are now
◈

CEO mindset

Being a CEO is not a title you get, it is a way of seeing the world you practise until it is natural. The shift from employee to founder happens in your thinking long before it happens on paper.

Ownership thinking, treat everything at Lucent as yours to learn from, not just yours to survive
Comfort with being the decision-maker, practise making calls without asking for permission or approval, even on small things
Read: The Hard Thing About Hard Things (Horowitz), Zero to One (Thiel), Shoe Dog (Knight)
Monthly founder review, 15 mins: what did I learn, what did I observe, what am I building toward

Appearance

The aesthetic

How you want to be seen before you have said a word. A CEO's visual language is deliberate, not default. This is yours.

01

Structured

Tailored fit. Clean cuts. Nothing that hangs wrong. Every piece chosen, not grabbed.

02

Minimal

One focal point per look. Quality reads from across the room. Quantity does not.

03

Authoritative

Colours and silhouettes that hold a room. Nothing apologetic. Nothing that shrinks.

04

Signature

Consistent enough to be recognisable. People know your aesthetic before they know your name.

White

The rule

Monochrome as your default. Navy or black with one neutral. Colour as a deliberate signal, not a filler. The palette should work in a Canary Wharf meeting and a conference keynote equally well.

Wardrobe signals

A well-fitted blazer that makes you feel like you own the room, not one that fits like you borrowed it
Trousers as your conference default: clean lines, nothing to fidget with, no hem worries on a stage
One quality signature accessory worn consistently, a watch, a pendant, or a ring. Consistency builds recognisability
Shoes you can stand in for six hours and still feel in control. Comfort and authority are not opposites
A structured bag that reads as intentional when it sits on a boardroom table, not an afterthought
