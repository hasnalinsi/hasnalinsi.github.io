The version of you that walks into the FP Advance conference and closes deals on day one of Amaea's launch is built now, not in 2028.

✦

Personal brand

The story of who you are needs to be consistent across every touchpoint — LinkedIn, conference introductions, how you write emails, how you show up in a room. You are already building this. The FP Advance speech and the Robin Powell interview are the foundation.

LinkedIn profile positioned as FCA compliance expert and future founder — not just employee
Post once a week on FCA compliance, Consumer Duty, or IFA industry topics — build authority before launch
Prepare a 2-minute founder story — why Amaea, why you, why now. Practised until it's effortless
Speaking reputation established — FP Advance, CISI talk already done
◆

Appearance & presence

How you look and carry yourself signals how you see yourself before you've said a word. A CEO's appearance is a deliberate choice — not vanity, strategy. Authoritative, polished, and entirely yours.

Define a signature style — a consistent aesthetic that reads as confident and professional in any room
Conference-ready wardrobe — know exactly what you wear to FP Advance, Intelliflo, CISI events
Physical presence — posture, eye contact, pacing. The gym is part of this. How you hold yourself changes how a room receives you
Headshots updated — professional photos that match the CEO you're becoming, not the employee you are now
◈

CEO mindset

Being a CEO isn't a title you get — it's a way of seeing the world you practise until it's natural. The shift from employee to founder happens in your thinking long before it happens on paper.

Ownership thinking — treat everything at Lucent as yours to learn from, not just yours to survive
Comfort with being the decision-maker — practise making calls without asking for permission or approval, even on small things
Read: The Hard Thing About Hard Things (Horowitz), Zero to One (Thiel), Shoe Dog (Knight)
Monthly founder review — 15 mins: what did I learn, what did I observe, what am I building toward
